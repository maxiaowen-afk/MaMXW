SELECTED WORKS

CONCEPTUAL FRAMEWORK

This project is a deeply personal look at how we leave certain versions of ourselves behind. The starting point is my one-way boarding pass from Harbin to Los Angeles. But for me, this flight wasn't just a change in geography; it marked a permanent shift in who I am. Back in Harbin, my life was on the ice. As a national team ice hockey player, my entire identity was built on speed, physical strength, and the freedom of the game. Today, my reality is very different. My body has changed, I've gained weight, and I can no longer physically play the sport I loved so much. That version of me exists only in the past. By placing this flight ticket next to just a few select old hockey photos, I want to explore this honest feeling of loss and transition. The goal isn't to create a cluttered timeline using every piece of material I have, but to keep it simple and focus on the sharp contrast. It’s about the quiet tension between the fast-paced athlete captured in those photos and my current, grounded reality, as I try to figure out who I am now without the sport that used to define me.

CONCEPTUAL FRAMEWORK

The Subject: A mass-produced "Minion" figure.

The Idea: Exploring the contrast between industrial perfection and biological absurdity. The Goal: To translate a 2D pop-culture icon into a structured 3D environment, focusing on the tension between its simple "pill" shape and complex mechanical accessories.
